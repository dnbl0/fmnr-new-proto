Why monitoring matters

Measuring restoration as it grows

To scale up the impact of FMNR and other land restoration efforts, we need to understand what's working, where, and why. Monitoring helps us capture change on the ground, learn from local experience, and make better decisions. It also helps communities and partners see the value of their efforts and tell powerful stories of restoration.

The FMNR Tracking Tool is built on Solstice — a free and open-source, cloud-based platform for mapping and monitoring restoration everywhere. Standardised indicators, surveys and site profiles make collecting meaningful data quick and easy, while remaining customisable to the needs of individual projects and organisations.

World Vision has invested in Solstice to develop site profiles, surveys, indicators and dashboards suited to the mapping and monitoring of FMNR and regreening sites. These tools are freely available to any user around the world.

What it tracks

The numbers behind the regreening

From a single regenerated stump to whole landscapes restored, the tool turns field observations into measurable, comparable indicators of change.

Trees Trees regenerated

The count of stumps, shoots and trees managed back to life across each site — the core measure of FMNR's "underground forest" coming back.

Hectares Area under FMNR

Site boundaries mapped in the field, with area calculated instantly in hectares or acres — solving a long-standing challenge in land restoration.

Species Species & diversity

Which species are regenerating and how diverse a site is becoming — a signal of ecosystem health and the benefits available to communities.

Survival Survival & growth rates

Repeat surveys over time show survival and growth, so practitioners can see what is working and where regrowth needs more protection.

Alongside the trees and the land, the tool also tracks the people — the number of participating farmers and households — and links field photographs and survey responses to each site so progress can be verified and shared.

How data is collected

From the field to the dashboard

Solstice combines a mobile app for field teams with a web portal for managers — built to work even where there is no connectivity or phone service.

Solstice App

Designed for field staff collecting data on the go, the app works offline so crucial information is never lost.

  • Capture field data and photographs
  • Conduct household surveys
  • Accurately map site boundaries
  • Instantaneously determine area (in hectares or acres)
  • Works in remote areas without connectivity

Solstice Web Portal

The portal lets project teams design surveys, manage data and visualise progress in real time.

  • Create customised survey forms
  • Manage and aggregate data across projects, regions and countries
  • Build dashboards and maps to track progress in real time
  • Easily add or remove users
  • Protect sensitive data
