1981

A young agronomist arrives in the Sahel

Tony Rinaudo arrives in the Maradi region of Niger as a missionary agronomist with Serving in Mission (SIM). He finds a landscape stripped bare — trees removed, soils degraded, and communities facing chronic food insecurity. His early planting efforts repeatedly fail in the hostile environment.

1983

The underground forest discovery

Driving through the fields, Tony notices living stumps — dormant root systems just below the surface, waiting to regenerate. He realises that rather than planting new trees from scratch, farmers could simply protect and nurture these existing root networks. FMNR as a practice is born from this moment of insight.

1990s

Niger's great green wall begins

Farmers across southern Niger adopt FMNR at scale — without waiting for top-down programmes. Satellite imagery later reveals that more than 5 million hectares of degraded land have been restored, and agricultural yields have increased significantly across the region.

2000s

World Vision and global expansion

Tony joins World Vision Australia and begins taking FMNR beyond the Sahel. The practice spreads to Ethiopia, Ghana, Senegal, and across Sub-Saharan Africa. International researchers and development organisations take note, publishing studies that confirm FMNR's impact on food security, biodiversity, and climate resilience.

2018

Right Livelihood Award — the "Alternative Nobel"

Tony Rinaudo is awarded the Right Livelihood Award — often called the "Alternative Nobel Prize" — in recognition of FMNR's transformative impact on farming communities and landscapes across Africa and beyond. The award brings global attention to the simplicity and scalability of the approach.

Today

26 countries. Millions of hectares. Growing.

FMNR is now practised in 26 countries across Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. Tens of millions of hectares have been restored. The FMNR Hub supports farmers, governments, and partners to spread and sustain the practice — making it one of the most cost-effective land restoration interventions in the world.
