Country · Ethiopia

Restoring Ethiopia's Highlands Through FMNR

Community-led restoration in the Humbo region has regenerated thousands of hectares of degraded woodland — delivering food security, income and carbon benefits for farming families.

Country overview

FMNR in Ethiopia

Ethiopia's Humbo Community Reforestation Programme is a landmark example of farmer-led restoration. Supported by community land rights agreements and carbon finance, it has become one of FMNR's most-cited success stories globally.

Landscape

Woodland Recovery

Over 10,000 hectares of degraded land brought back to life through community-led FMNR practice in the Humbo region alone — with visible tree regeneration within the first season.

Livelihoods

Community Income

Restored trees provide firewood, fodder, fruit and timber — directly improving household income and food security for thousands of farming families across the programme area.

Finance

Carbon Finance

The Humbo project was among the first FMNR programmes to access carbon finance through an Emissions Reduction Purchase Agreement — providing communities with a long-term income stream.

01

The Humbo Project

The Humbo Community Reforestation Programme established community management committees with formal land rights, enabling farmers to protect and restore woodland that had previously been degraded by open grazing and cutting.

  • Community land rights secured across the project area
  • Local management committees established
  • FMNR practice introduced across 10,000+ hectares
  • Annual monitoring and benefit-sharing mechanisms

02

Beyond Humbo

The lessons and frameworks developed at Humbo are being applied across Ethiopia's highland regions, with government and NGO partners integrating FMNR into national restoration and food security programmes.

  • Replication across neighbouring regions
  • Integration with national restoration targets
  • Field officer training at scale
  • Evidence base shared with global restoration community
