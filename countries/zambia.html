Country overview

FMNR in Zambia

Zambia's FMNR story is anchored in community-led pilots that demonstrate the practice's power to deliver climate resilience, alternative livelihoods and strong community organisation. The Sinda region has emerged as a flagship example of what is possible at community scale.

Climate

Climate Resilience

FMNR-restored tree cover buffers against drought and flood extremes — building the climate resilience that Zambian communities urgently need as rainfall patterns become less predictable.

Community

Community Organisation

Zambia's pilots are led by organised community groups — including women's groups, schools and farmer cooperatives — creating durable local governance that outlasts the initial programme cycle.

Enterprise

Alternative Livelihoods

Restored trees open new income streams including sustainable timber, beekeeping and fruit production — reducing dependence on charcoal and creating more diversified household economies.

01

The Sinda Pilot

The Sinda pilot in Eastern Province established a community-driven model for FMNR adoption — led by women's groups, school communities and farmer cooperatives who collectively manage restoration across their landscape.

  • Community mobilisation and consent-building process
  • Tree inventory and FMNR baseline established
  • Practice introduced across pilot households
  • Women's group leadership in monitoring and governance

02

Replication and Growth

Lessons from Sinda are being documented and shared nationally, with government and NGO partners exploring expansion to new communities in Eastern Province and alignment with Zambia's national restoration commitments.

  • Sinda lessons documented and shared nationally
  • NGO and government partner onboarding
  • Extension to new communities in Eastern Province
  • Integration with national restoration targets
