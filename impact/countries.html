Where We Work

Birthplace of FMNR

Niger

The greening of the Sahel. More than 200 million trees regenerated across 5 million hectares — the largest positive environmental transformation in Africa, and where FMNR began.

Read the Niger story →
East Africa

Ethiopia

At Humbo, FMNR restored 2,700+ hectares of degraded hillside — Africa's first large-scale forestry carbon project, reviving biodiversity, water and farm incomes.

East Africa

Kenya

Across Baringo, West Pokot and the eastern drylands, farmer groups are bringing back trees, fodder and food security in some of the country's harshest landscapes.

West Africa

Ghana

In the Talensi District, the difference between 2010 and 2016 is striking — bare farmland transformed into wooded, productive landscapes through farmer-led regeneration.

Uganda

Catalyst projects are spreading FMNR across degraded farmland, helping communities cope with land pressure, population growth and a changing climate.

Southern Africa

Zambia

With forest loss running at roughly 250,000 hectares a year, rural communities are turning to FMNR to protect soils, restore woodland and secure their livelihoods.

And Beyond

Part of the Great Green Wall

FMNR is a cornerstone of Africa's Great Green Wall — the ambition to restore a belt of land across the Sahel from Senegal to the Horn. Senegal, Mali, Burkina Faso, Tanzania, Somalia and many more are adopting the practice as the movement spreads, farmer to farmer.
