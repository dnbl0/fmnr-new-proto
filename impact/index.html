FMNR Impact

EVIDENCE OF REAL-WORLD IMPACT.

Four decades. Eight countries. One clear conclusion — Farmer Managed Natural Regeneration (FMNR) produces rapid, measurable improvements to trees, soil, income, food security, child wellbeing and gender equality.

Land & trees restored

FMNR produces rapid, measurable improvements in tree cover, soil fertility and erosion — transforming degraded farmland back into productive, biodiverse landscapes.

Humbo, Ethiopia

The Humbo project is one of the few FMNR studies with a true comparison group. In project areas, 83% of participants reported improved soil quality compared to 22% in comparison areas. For erosion reduction the gap was 85% vs 22% — a 63 percentage point difference attributable to FMNR.

"Before there were no trees, it was bare degraded land... After protection, the trees started growing fast, wildlife started coming back, the temperature has gone down, and rainfall is getting better." — Child members of environment club, Humbo, Ethiopia

Income & livelihoods

FMNR generates measurable income gains for participating households — through improved crop yields, wood and forest products, and reduced expenditure on inputs previously purchased from outside the farm.

Food, families & futures

FMNR's effects ripple beyond the farm: fewer months without food, healthier children, women with more time and agency, and communities with stronger social trust.

−0.26 months of food shortage per year (average across sites). Participant households were 8 percentage points more likely to provide well for children.

Carbon & sequestration

FMNR is a verified, audited climate solution. Two Ethiopian projects alone have sequestered over 276,000 tonnes of CO₂.

  • 181k tonnes CO₂ sequestered — Humbo (CDM certified)
  • 94k tonnes CO₂ sequestered — Soddo (Gold Standard)

Country spotlights

East Africa

Kenya, Rwanda, Tanzania, Uganda — 2012–2017: 852,000 direct beneficiaries; 2,728 ha FMNR coverage; +22–24pp income increase likelihood.

Ethiopia — Humbo & Soddo

73,974 direct beneficiaries; 276k tCO₂ sequestered; $638k carbon credit revenue.

Ghana — Talensi

12,000 direct beneficiaries; 567 ha FMNR coverage; social ROI per household $655–887/yr.
