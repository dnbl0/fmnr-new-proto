Four Decades of Measurable Change

FMNR produces rapid, measurable improvements across tree cover, soil health, income, food security, child wellbeing and carbon sequestration — backed by independent research from eight countries.

Explore the evidence Download impact report

The Evidence

Real outcomes, independently verified

Four decades. Eight countries. One clear conclusion — FMNR produces rapid, measurable improvements to trees, soil, income, food security, child wellbeing and gender equality.

These outcomes come from independent evaluations using control groups, satellite data and household surveys conducted across Africa and Asia. The evidence base is rigorous, diverse, and growing — and it consistently points in one direction.

"Before there were no trees, it was bare degraded land… After protection, the trees started growing fast, wildlife started coming back."

— Community member, Humbo, Ethiopia

Domains of Impact

Four areas of proven change

Independent research consistently shows FMNR driving meaningful outcomes across four interconnected domains. Each builds on the others — restoring land restores livelihoods, which strengthens families and communities.

01

Land & Trees

Measurable improvements in tree cover, soil fertility, erosion control and biodiversity — evidenced across multiple countries and landscapes.

02

Income & Livelihoods

FMNR households consistently report higher income, reduced expenditure on inputs, and diversified livelihood options through wood and forest products.

03

Food, Families & Futures

Fewer hungry months, better child health, greater women's agency, and stronger community cohesion — FMNR's benefits ripple far beyond the farm.

04

Carbon & Climate

Verified, audited carbon sequestration with carbon credit revenues flowing directly back to communities — a proven climate finance mechanism.

01

Land & Trees Restored

FMNR produces rapid, measurable improvements in tree cover, soil fertility and erosion control. In Humbo, Ethiopia — one of few FMNR studies with a true comparison group — 83% of participants reported improved soil quality vs 22% in comparison areas. For erosion reduction: 85% vs 22%, a 63 percentage-point difference attributable to FMNR.

  • 83% of FMNR farmers report improved soil quality (Humbo, Ethiopia)
  • 5M+ hectares regenerated in Niger alone
  • Canopy cover increased by an average of 40% within 3 years
  • Biodiversity returns alongside tree cover
Income gains — East Africa study 7:42

02

Income & Livelihoods

FMNR generates measurable income gains through improved crop yields, wood and forest products, and reduced expenditure on inputs. Across Kenya, Rwanda, Tanzania and Uganda (2012–2017), FMNR households were 23 percentage points more likely to report income increases than comparison households.

  • +23pp more likely to report income increase (East Africa)
  • Average income gain of $72 per household (Sahel research)
  • $638,000 in carbon credit revenue over 10 years (Humbo)
  • Social ROI of $655–$887 per household per year (Ghana, Talensi)

In the Field

Impact at ground level

03

Food, Families & Futures

FMNR's effects ripple far beyond the farm. Research across multiple sites shows FMNR households experience fewer hungry months, better child health outcomes, and women with more time and agency. Participant households experience on average 0.26 fewer months of food shortage per year.

  • −0.26 months of food shortage per year on average
  • 8pp more likely to adequately provide for children
  • Women save 2+ hours daily on firewood collection
  • Stronger community social cohesion and trust
  • Gender equity improves as women gain resource rights

04

Carbon & Climate

FMNR is a verified, audited climate solution. Two Ethiopian projects alone — Humbo (CDM certified) and Soddo (Gold Standard) — have sequestered over 276,000 tonnes of CO₂ equivalent, generating significant carbon credit revenue that flows directly back to participating communities.

  • 181,000 tCO₂ sequestered — Humbo (CDM certified)
  • 94,000 tCO₂ sequestered — Soddo (Gold Standard)
  • Carbon finance can fund long-term community land stewardship
  • FMNR contributes to NDCs and national climate commitments

Country Spotlights

Where FMNR is changing lives

FMNR has been independently evaluated in eight or more countries, with programmes currently active across 26 nations. From Niger's Sahel to Ethiopia's highlands, Kenya's drylands to Ghana's northern savanna — each context brings unique challenges and remarkable results.

Country-level evidence consistently demonstrates that FMNR works across diverse agroecological zones, land tenure systems, and cultural contexts — making it one of the most transferable land restoration approaches available.
